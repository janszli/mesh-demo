About Me

Hi, I’m Jun — an independent artist focused on traditional media, anatomy, and figure drawing. I build my work on observation and practice, using charcoal, pen and ink, and digital tools to study the body, gesture, and rhythm of form. My drawings aim for clarity and structure without losing the energy of a live mark.

I was born in Changsha, Hunan Province, China, and moved to the U.S. as a child. I grew up in a small town in New Hampshire, where sketchbooks and library art books became my classroom. Eighteen years of drawing later, I’m still driven by the same curiosity: how light wraps a form, how a line carries weight, and how a pose can tell a story without words.

I began as a comic artist, learning pacing, staging, and clear visual storytelling. That foundation led me deeper into anatomy and figure work — long and short poses, structure studies, and daily sketch sessions. Today my process blends traditional composition with disciplined study: bold charcoal for mass and value, pen and ink for edge and texture, and digital for planning, refinement, and color notes.

My influences range from classic figure painters and illustrators to contemporary draftsmanship. I’m interested in drawings that feel lived-in: honest proportions, decisive marks, and color kept in service of form. Whether the piece is an anatomical study or a narrative figure drawing, my goal is the same — clarity, intent, and a human presence on the page.

I work independently and release personal studies, prints, and finished drawings. I’m open to select collaborations and commissions related to figure work, portraits, and narrative pieces. If my approach resonates with you, I’d love to share more — thank you for looking.
